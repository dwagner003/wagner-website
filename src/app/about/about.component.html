Entering college I never planned on becoming a software engineer. The first semester I stumbled into an Intro to Computer Science and I stuck with it. Years later, lots of helpful people, and expereinces I am greatful for I find myself enjoying a career in software.

2018

Four years of college came to an end. Along the way were experiences working with athletics, a role in student government, participation in the leadership program, and a service learning opportunity in Nicaragua. I graduated from Regis University with a Bachelors in Computer Science in May and was lucky enough to have a full stack software engineering position lined up. Outside of school I spent my free time refereeing soccer matches of all levels (youth, adult, professional). August of this year I started pursing my Masters in Software Engineering at Regis while continuing to work full time.

2019

I continued with my Masters and decided on an emphasis in Web Development. Refereeing was still an active part of my life but multiple injuries slowed the down quite a bit. Professionally work work going great. Every day I was learning something new and was challenged to learn new skills with C#, SQL, AngularJS, and CoffeeScript. In December I finished my Masters in Software Engineering. “Exhausted” would be an understatement.

2020

Being done with school was a weight off the shoulders. In my free time I have started reading a mix of technical books to develop new skills along with some more relaxing fiction. Quarantine of 2020 arrived which eliminated the ability to referee. I picked up running seriously for the first time, built a desktop PC, and started to build this website. Work continues to go well and I am just starting to work with micro-services in AWS.
